Spotlight on Essential Contributors: An Exclusive Interview with a Sanitary Worker




Interview date: 09 Novemeber,2023

My name is Bilal. I have been working as a sanitary worker for past 7 years. My primary responsibility is road cleaning cooperative society. It's a tough job, but someone has to do it. When I think of challenges, the first thing that comes to mind is the lack of awareness among the public. Many people still don't understand the importance of keeping our streets clean. They throw trash on the roads without thinking about the consequences. It's disheartening to see how little some people care about their surroundings.

It makes our job much harder. We have to deal with all kinds of waste, from plastic bottles to food wrappers. Sometimes people even dump larger items like furniture or electronic appliances on the roadside. It not only slows us down but also poses safety risks. Broken glass, sharp objects. We have to be extra careful.

Another big challenge is the weather. Whether it's scorching heat in the summer or heavy rain during the monsoon season, we're out there doing our job. The extreme weather conditions take a toll on our health. It's not easy to work in such conditions, but the streets need to be cleaned regardless.

The working conditions could be better. We often lack proper equipment, like gloves and masks, which are essential for our safety. Also, the cleaning equipment we use is sometimes outdated, making our job more difficult. I wish there was more investment in providing us with the right tools for the job.

Despite all the difficulties, I take pride in knowing that my work contributes to maintaining a clean and healthy environment for everyone. I believe that every little effort counts, and by keeping the roads clean, I'm playing my part in making our city a better place.
